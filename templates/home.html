Predictor Inputs Overview

This input represents the time of day the accident occurred, broken into categories such as Early Morning, Morning Rush, Midday, etc.

The specific day of the week when the accident happened. Each day might have different traffic conditions, which can affect accident severity.

This input refers to the age group of the driver involved in the accident. The age of the driver can influence driving patterns and accident risks.

This input refers to the age group of any casualties involved in the accident. Certain age groups may be more vulnerable in accidents.

This input indicates how many vehicles were involved in the accident. More vehicles typically increase the severity of the accident.

The number of people injured or killed in the accident. This variable is critical in determining the overall severity.

This input refers to the type of junction where the accident occurred. Junction types such as T-junction, Crossroads, and Roundabouts may have different accident risks.

This input refers to the geographical area where the accident took place, such as residential areas, urban areas, or rural areas.

This input describes the lighting conditions at the time of the accident. Daylight, darkness, and street lighting can all influence accident severity.

This input refers to the weather conditions at the time of the accident, including normal, rainy, foggy, or snowy weather, which can all affect driving and accident severity.
